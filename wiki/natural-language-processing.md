# Natural Language Processing

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a field at the intersection of computer science, artificial intelligence, and linguistics. It focuses on enabling computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language in a meaningful way.

## Core Tasks

### Text Processing

- **Tokenization**: Breaking text into words, subwords, or characters
- **Stemming & Lemmatization**: Reducing words to their root form
- **Part-of-Speech Tagging**: Identifying grammatical roles of words
- **Named Entity Recognition (NER)**: Identifying names, dates, locations, and other entities

### Understanding

- **Sentiment Analysis**: Determining the emotional tone of text
- **Text Classification**: Categorizing documents into predefined groups
- **Question Answering**: Extracting answers from a body of text
- **Semantic Similarity**: Measuring how alike two pieces of text are in meaning

### Generation

- **Machine Translation**: Converting text between languages
- **Text Summarization**: Producing concise summaries of longer texts
- **Text Generation**: Creating human-like text from prompts
- **Dialogue Systems**: Building conversational agents and chatbots

## Language Models

Modern NLP is dominated by large language models (LLMs) based on the Transformer architecture:

- **BERT** (2018): Bidirectional encoder for understanding tasks
- **GPT Series** (2018-present): Autoregressive models for text generation
- **T5** (2019): Unified text-to-text framework
- **LLaMA** (2023): Open-weight large language models

These models are pre-trained on vast text corpora and then fine-tuned for specific tasks, a paradigm known as **transfer learning**.

## Challenges

NLP faces several ongoing challenges including handling ambiguity and context, understanding sarcasm and figurative language, maintaining factual accuracy in generation, reducing bias in training data, and supporting low-resource languages with limited training data.

## Applications

NLP powers many everyday technologies: search engines, virtual assistants (Siri, Alexa), email spam filters, auto-complete and grammar checkers, content moderation systems, and real-time translation services.
